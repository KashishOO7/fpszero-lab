# Deep Tech — The Frontier

> These are the domains where the physics is being actively rewritten. Understanding them now, before they become mainstream, is the leverage point.

---

## What Belongs Here

Deep tech, in the fpszero sense, is any domain where:

1. **The underlying physics or math is non-obvious** — you cannot understand it by analogy alone
2. **The security implications are underappreciated** — most practitioners don't see the threat or opportunity yet
3. **The timeline to relevance is measurable** — not science fiction, but not yet widely deployed

## The First Principles Lens

Most "deep tech" content is either too hyped ("quantum will break everything tomorrow") or too dismissive ("quantum is useless"). The truth requires understanding the *actual physics and math*, not the narrative.

For each domain, the questions are:

1. What is the fundamental mechanism? (Physics/math layer)
2. What can it do that classical systems cannot? (Capability layer)
3. What are the realistic timelines? (Engineering layer)
4. What are the security implications — both attack and defense? (Threat model layer)
